Sashimi

Sashimi is simply raw fish eaten with soy sauce and wasabi

There are a plethora of different types of sashimi. The most common ones include salmon, tuna, yellowtail, shrimp, and more.

Sashimi is usually eaten dipped in soy sauce and with a little bit of wasabi on it. You can usually find it on salads, or on a plate by itself.

Ingredients

  • choice of fish
    • fish should be skinned and filleted
  • soy sauce
  • wasabi

Steps

  1. cut the fish in a rectangular slice about 0.5 - 1cm
  2. put wasabi on the fish before dipping in soy sauce
    • never mix the wasabi in the soy sauce
    • make sure you don't put too much soy sauce in the dish
  3. serve and enjoy raw fish
