Curry Rice

This is a Japanese dish which was inspired by traditional Indian Curry.

This is a savory dish commonly eaten by Japanese people. It became popular in Japan in the early 1900s in households and now have many restaurants which specialize in serving this type of dish.

One of the most popular curry rice restaurants is called Cocoichiban. At this restaurant, you can order beef based, pork based, and even chicken based curry. They also have a plethora of toppings such as a variety of vegetables, seafood, beef or pork, and even chicken cutlet! To spice things up even more, you can choose the level of spiciness which goes up to 10. Still, Japanese people prefer homemade curry rice. Let's learn how to make it!

Cooking time: Approx 60 mins

Ingredients

  • 4 cubes of curry roo
  • 2 potatoes
  • 1 whole onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 100g beef used for stew
  • 200g white rice

Preparation

  1. cube the beef
  2. cube the potatoes and carrots
  3. cut the onion in half and chop each piece of the onion into 1/4ths

Cooking

  1. stiry fry the beef until light brown and remove
  2. stiry fry the potatoes in the same pan until opaque
  3. add the beef, carrots, and onions
  4. stiry fry everything until soft
  5. add 500ml of water and let simmer for 25 mins
  6. add 4 cubes of curry roo and turn off the heat
  7. after 5 mins, let it simmer on low heat for another 5 mins and stir occasionally
  8. serve over 200g of rice as one serving
